Understanding ISO

ISO controls your camera’s sensitivity to light. Increasing the ISO makes your photos brighter in low-light settings, but also introduces more grain or “noise” to the image.

Increasing ISO can help brighten a photo in darker conditions, as seen in this example.

But higher ISO also means more “noise” or grain, which can reduce image quality—notice the grainy texture here.
