ISO and Grain

When you increase ISO to make your pictures brighter, you also introduce grain, which is called "noise." While a higher ISO helps capture images in low light, it comes at the cost of reduced image quality.

To get the best quality, you should always keep your ISO levels as low as possible, and instead adjust your aperture or shutter speed first to brighten the photo. A good ISO range for clean, sharp images is usually between 100 and 1600, depending on your lighting conditions and camera.
