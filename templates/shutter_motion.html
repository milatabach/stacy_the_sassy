Shutter Speed and Motion

Shutter speed has a major effect on how motion appears in your photographs. A fast shutter speed, like 1/1000th of a second, can freeze fast-moving subjects, capturing them in sharp detail without blur. A slow shutter speed, like 1/30th of a second or slower, will blur moving subjects, creating a sense of motion in the image.

Choosing the right shutter speed depends on the effect you want: freeze the action or show the movement.
