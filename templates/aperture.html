Aperture Recap

Aperture controls how wide the camera’s lens opens to let in light. It’s measured in f-stops like f/2.8 or f/16. A wider aperture (lower f-stop) lets in more light and creates a shallow depth of field, blurring the background. A narrower aperture (higher f-stop) keeps more of the image in focus.
